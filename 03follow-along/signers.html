Who Were the Signers

Famuous Signers

Some of the most famous signers of the Declaration of Independence include

John Hancock
As the President of the Continental Congress, Hancock is the most famous signer due to his large, bold signature. His name became synonymous with the idea of signing one's name to an important document.
Thomas Jefferson
The primary author of the Declaration, Jefferson was a key figure in the early development of the United States. He later served as the third President of the U.S.
Benjamin Franklin
A renowned polymath, Franklin was one of the leading figures of the American Enlightenment and played a crucial role in securing French support during the Revolutionary War.
John Adams
A strong advocate for independence, Adams was a key member of the Continental Congress and later served as the second President of the United States.
Samuel Adams
A cousin of John Adams, Samuel was a firebrand revolutionary and one of the key leaders in the push for independence.
Richard Henry Lee
He introduced the resolution calling for independence in the Continental Congress, which led to the Declaration being drafted.
Roger Sherman
A Founding Father who also helped draft the Articles of Confederation, Sherman is the only person to have signed all four major American documents: the Continental Association, the Declaration of Independence, the Articles of Confederation, and the U.S. Constitution.
Francis Lewis
A wealthy merchant and public official, Lewis was a signatory and an active patriot.

Each of These Individuals played a significant roles in the founding of the United States and is remembered for their courage in signing the Declaration, an act considered treasonus by the British Crown.
